Introduction to PET

What Positron Emission Tomography (PET) is and how it works: PET is an imaging technique that uses positron-emitting radionuclides to visualize and measure metabolic processes in the body.

Basic principles of PET imaging: PET involves the injection of radiotracers, which emit positrons that interact with electrons, producing gamma rays detected by the scanner. This process allows for the visualization of functional processes within the body, particularly in the brain.
