Practical Guide and Tutorials

1. MRI Setup and Preparation:

Patient Preparation:

  • Safety Screening: Perform a comprehensive safety screening to identify contraindications, such as metallic implants, pacemakers, or other electronic devices that could interfere with MRI. Use standardized questionnaires and thorough verbal questioning to ensure all potential risks are identified.
  • Positioning and Comfort: Ensure the patient is positioned correctly and comfortably on the MRI table. Provide ear protection to mitigate noise exposure and use cushions or supports to prevent discomfort during the scan.
  • Instructions to Avoid Movement: Clearly explain to patients the importance of remaining still during the scan to avoid motion artifacts. Offer tips and practice sessions for anxious or claustrophobic patients to help them remain calm and still.

Equipment Setup:

  • Coil Selection and Placement: Select the appropriate coil based on the body part being imaged. Properly position and secure the coil to optimize signal reception and image quality.
  • Adjusting Scanner Settings: Adjust scanner settings such as field strength, repetition time (TR), echo time (TE), and flip angles based on the imaging protocol. Ensure these settings are optimized for the specific clinical or research question.
  • Calibration Procedures: Regularly calibrate the MRI scanner to ensure accurate and consistent imaging results. Follow the manufacturer's guidelines for calibration frequency and procedures.

2. MRI Protocols and Sequences:

Protocol Selection:

Choose MRI protocols and sequences that are appropriate for the clinical question or research objective. Consider factors such as tissue type, desired contrast, and anatomical area. For example, T1-weighted imaging is ideal for anatomical detail, while T2-weighted imaging is better for identifying pathology like edema or tumors.
